# Proof Notes

Discrete mathematics uses proof to move beyond finite examples.

## Common proof forms

- direct proof;
- proof by cases;
- proof by contrapositive;
- proof by contradiction;
- mathematical induction;
- strong induction;
- bijective proof;
- invariant proof.

## Validation questions

- Are all cases exhausted?
- Is the induction base case correct?
- Is the inductive step valid?
- Is the bijection one-to-one and onto?
- Does an algorithm preserve its invariant?
- Is the conclusion stronger than the evidence supports?
