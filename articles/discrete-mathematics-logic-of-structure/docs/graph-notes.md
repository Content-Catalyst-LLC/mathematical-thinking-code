# Graph Notes

Graphs represent objects and relationships as vertices and edges.

## Key distinction

A graph drawing is not the graph itself. The graph is defined by its vertices and edges. A layout may suggest distance, centrality, or clustering visually, but these meanings must be defined mathematically before they are interpreted.

## Audit questions

- Is the graph directed or undirected?
- Are multiple edges allowed?
- Are loops allowed?
- What does an edge mean?
- Is edge weight meaningful?
- Are isolated vertices included?
- Does visual distance mean anything?
