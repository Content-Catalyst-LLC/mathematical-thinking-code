# Validation Plan

## Dataset checks

- Confirm every historical practice has object, medium, method, meaning, and caution.
- Confirm every historiographic risk has a mitigation.
- Confirm every transmission distinguishes preserved and transformed content.
- Confirm every notation item includes an anachronism warning.
- Confirm every proof style includes authority basis and limitation.
- Confirm canon risks name a responsible response.

## Historical review

- Distinguish mathematical validity from historical practice.
- Distinguish original notation from modern reconstruction.
- Avoid Eurocentric single-line narratives.
- Attend to source survival and institutional power.
- Include marginalized, practical, oral, pedagogical, and material mathematical labor where evidence supports it.

## Mathematical review

- Preserve mathematical content accurately.
- Distinguish proof, procedure, model, simulation, computation, and formal verification.
- Distinguish formal correctness from social interpretation.

## Responsible-use note

This scaffold is for structured historical and mathematical reflection. It does not replace specialist history of mathematics, primary-source scholarship, philology, manuscript study, mathematical proof, formal verification, or ethical review.
