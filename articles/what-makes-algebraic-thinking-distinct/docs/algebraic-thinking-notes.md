# Algebraic Thinking Notes

This article treats algebraic thinking as reasoning about quantity, relation, structure, and transformation.

## Core distinctions

- algebra is not merely arithmetic with letters;
- variables have multiple roles;
- expressions are mathematical objects;
- equations can be conditions, identities, relations, models, or geometric descriptions;
- transformations must preserve meaning under stated assumptions;
- functions express dependence and change;
- algebraic proof uses symbols to reason about general cases.

## Professional use

The repository can support algebra education, symbolic computation, expression-tree design, transformation audits, proof-status tracking, and formalization planning.
