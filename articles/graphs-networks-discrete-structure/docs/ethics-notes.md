# Ethics Notes

Graphs make relationships visible, but visibility is not neutral.

## Responsible questions

- What counts as a node?
- What counts as an edge?
- Who defined the graph?
- What evidence supports each relation?
- What data is missing?
- What does centrality measure?
- What harms could follow from graph-based inference?
- Could association be mistaken for causation, suspicion, trust, or value?

Graphs can clarify connection, but they can also encode power, surveillance, exclusion, false association, or biased measurement.
