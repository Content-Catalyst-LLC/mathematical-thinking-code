# Responsible Computing Notes

Mathematical rigor is necessary but not sufficient.

## Responsible questions

- What is the specification?
- Who defined the objective?
- What assumptions are encoded?
- What edge cases are excluded?
- What groups may be affected?
- What does the output mean?
- How is uncertainty communicated?
- What can go wrong even if the program is formally correct?

A system can satisfy a formal specification and still be harmful if the specification is unjust, incomplete, or poorly grounded.
