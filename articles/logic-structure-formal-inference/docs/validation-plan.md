# Validation Plan

## Truth tables

- Verify truth-functional definitions for implication, equivalence, conjunction, disjunction, and negation.
- Confirm that implication and contrapositive agree in all truth-table rows.
- Confirm that converse and inverse are not generally equivalent to the original implication.

## Quantifier workflows

- Track the domain of discourse explicitly.
- Distinguish universal, existential, unique-existence, pointwise, and uniform quantifier forms.
- Link counterexamples to the precise universal claim they refute.

## Derivation graphs

- Confirm every dependency edge has a source and target.
- Detect cycles where derivation dependency should be acyclic.
- Export topological order when possible.
- Distinguish premises, rule applications, and conclusions.

## Formalization readiness

- Identify definitions, propositions, inference rules, and target proof systems.
- Keep informal explanation separate from formal derivation.
- Confirm that formal statements match intended mathematical meaning.

## Responsible use

Formal validity depends on premises and formalization choices. In applied contexts, assumptions, specifications, and domain relevance still require expert and ethical review.
