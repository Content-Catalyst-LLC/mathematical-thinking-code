# Proof-System Notes

Formal inference can be organized through different proof systems.

## Natural deduction

Natural deduction uses introduction and elimination rules for connectives and quantifiers. It often resembles ordinary proof structure.

## Sequent calculus

Sequent calculus represents inference as transformations between contexts of assumptions and conclusions. It is useful for proof theory and structural analysis.

## Hilbert systems

Hilbert-style systems use axiom schemas and a small number of inference rules. They are compact but less readable for many learners.

## Type theory

Type-theoretic systems connect propositions and proofs to types and terms. This is central to many proof assistants and constructive formalization workflows.

## Resolution

Resolution-based systems support automated theorem proving through refutation search.
