# Historiography Notes

The history of mathematical thinking should not be presented as a single linear march from “primitive” calculation to modern formalism.

## Responsible principles

- Include multiple global traditions.
- Distinguish original notation from modern reconstruction.
- Recognize procedure, diagram, proof, commentary, computation, and formalization as different media of mathematical thought.
- Avoid treating surviving elite texts as the entire history.
- Attend to transmission, translation, pedagogy, institutions, instruments, and collective labor.
- Recognize the power of modern proof assistants without treating them as the end of mathematics.
