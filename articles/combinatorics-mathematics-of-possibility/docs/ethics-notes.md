# Ethics Notes

Combinatorics can clarify possibility, but it can also hide assumptions.

## Responsible questions

- What possibilities are included?
- What possibilities are excluded?
- Who defined the constraints?
- Are cases overlapping?
- Are counts being interpreted as probabilities?
- Are all outcomes equally likely?
- Are rare but consequential possibilities included?
- Does the possibility space reflect affected communities?
- Is optimization using the right objective?

Combinatorial possibility is not the same as likelihood, feasibility, desirability, or justice.
